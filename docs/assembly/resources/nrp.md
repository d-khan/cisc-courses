# National Research Platform (NRP)

## __What is NRP?__

The NRP is a community-owned research and education platform connecting researchers and educators to foster
collaboration, accelerate innovation, and share resources. Supported by over 50 institutions, including leadership from
UC San Diego, the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, and the Massachusetts Green High Performance Computing Center, the NRP
provides access to cutting-edge technologies in AI, high-performance computing, data storage, and networking.

---

## __Why Use NRP in the CISC 211 course?__

Assembly language programs are highly dependent on the underlying hardware architecture and operating system. To ensure
a consistent lab environment for all students, the labs in this course are developed using the Intel x86 32-bit
architecture and the Linux operating system.

The National Research Platform (NRP) provides access to a Linux environment where you can write, assemble, and execute
your programs. Before beginning the labs, please watch the following video for instructions on setting up and using the
Linux environment:

[VIDEO: How to execute an Assembly Language code?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4IZSh1WVyi8)
